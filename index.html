Hi, I'm Seah. I'm the UX designer for MyFonts. A lot of times when I meet other designers, their response is, “but MyFonts already has pretty good UX, what is there for you to do?”

And it's true, a lot of my work isn't visible on the customer-facing side of the site at all. We've spent a lot of time recently improving our foundry-side processes, which includes creating tools I designed for our foundries and our own employees to use. More on this later.

As for the main site, over the past year I've made various small design improvements such as a redesign of the button styles, the addition of a live web font preview UI, and added more comprehensive licensing information to the font family pages. With a site the size of MyFonts, incremental design improvements really work best. Coming in 2014: MyFonts goes responsive! Plus some big new site features. It's not a redesign, it's an evolution of the site. (That's just what I say to keep the developers calm. It's actually a full redesign.)

Read on to learn what else I've been working on.

  • Foundry Tools

    product design lead · information architecture · visual design

    A work in progress, Foundry Tools will eventually be a full system for foundries to manage their presence on MyFonts. I designed the information architecture of this site from the ground up to address several needs for MyFonts staff and foundries who work with us. The project is fairly broad in scope. When released, it will provide a full-fledged back end for the foundries to use for tasks such as uploading a new font, checking that it will be displayed correctly, and sending it to us, filling out a public foundry profile, and viewing sales reports. Foundry Tools will go live for foundries we work with sometime in 2014. For now, you can preview the look and feel of Foundry Tools by checking out the Prospectus.

  • TypeCon 2013 Sponsor T-Shirts

    art direction

    For TypeCon Portland, I wanted to go in the opposite direction of most sponsors and create a series of t-shirts that was focused on illustration rather than typography or lettering. My concept for the shirts was a literal interpretation of the pangrams we use to show off fonts on our site. As you probably know, a pangram is a phrase containing every letter in the alphabet, which always ends up being a surreal phrase.
